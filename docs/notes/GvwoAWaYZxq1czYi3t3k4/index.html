Factor Models

This section gives a broad overview over factor models.

Most of this is more focused on giving a historical overview, and as such as a higher focus on Classical Factor Models. More more relevant and up to date literature, see Static Factor Models (PCA), or Dynamic Factor Models (Dynamic PCA).

Model Notation

We begin with the simplest factor model representation:

Xit=Cit+eitX_{it} = C_{it} + e_{it}Xit​=Cit​+eit​

where XitX_{it}Xit​ is decomposed into common component CitC_{it}Cit​ that is orthogonal to the idiosyncratic error eite_{it}eit​.

If eite_{it}eit​ is assumed to strictly independent (mutually orthogonal across all i,ti, ti,t), this leads to the so called "exact" or "strong factor model" initially considered by Geweke and Sargent and Sims. Although this, in conjunction with a standard gaussian assumption on the error terms leads to straightfoward estimation via ML/Kalman filter, it is understandably completely unrealistic.
