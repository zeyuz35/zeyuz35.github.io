SFM Break Theory

Bates et al (2013)

Bates et al is the first (and only) paper which establishes specified conditions on how large structural breaks can be allowed to be, without affecting the consistency of PCA. As such, it can be viewed in general as an attempt to see how far the original Bai and Ng regularity conditions can be loosened.

Note however, that this paper was produced before the development of the equivalent representation theorem, and as such is less useful is determining the precise behaviour of what happens in the presence of a structural break.

They achieve this by focusing on the error term in the usual DFM model setup:

Xit=λit′Ft+eit,X_{it} = \lambda_{it}' F_t + e_{it},Xit​=λit′​Ft​+eit​,

where hNTh_{NT}hNT​ is a deterministic scalar that may depend on NNN and/or TTT. Subbing this back into the general setup yields:

Bates et al focus on three specific examples which are thought to be of particular interest.

White noise: all ξit\xi_{it}ξit​ are i.i.d. across iii and ttt, and Λt\Lambda_tΛt​ is simple Λ0\Lambda_0Λ0​ plus some uncorrelated noise.

Random walk: The ξit\xi_{it}ξit​ are each random walk processes, and as such the factor loadings each evolve over time as uncorrelated random walks.

Single large break: The ξt\xi_tξt​ is simply a sudden shift parameter:

ξt={0for regime 1Δfor regime 2,\xi_t = \begin{cases} 0 \quad \text{for regime 1} \\ \Delta \quad \text{for regime 2}, \end{cases}ξt​={0for regime 1Δfor regime 2,​

where Δ\DeltaΔ is the shift parameter. Note that this case corresponds exactly to the type of break considered by Breitung and Eickmeier, which can be retroactively classified as being observationally equivalent to the emergence of r new factors, and hence results in a a DFM with double the number of factors.
