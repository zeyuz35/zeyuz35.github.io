
A more recent and unrelated strand of literature is the use of non-parametric approaches to model and test for possible structural instability.

## Su and Wang (2017)

This is a test based on the *common components*. Note that this is a little difficult to interpret, and is arguably at odds with what the null hypothesis is supposed to be.
## Su and Wang (2020)

This is refined version of the previous paper, and is now actually based on the factor loadings themselves. Unfortunately, the empirical study was based on financial data instead of macroeconomic data.


## Notes

Su and Wang for their first paper produce plots of time varying factor loadings and their associated confidence intervals, however, it is unclear how exactly these confidence intervals are constructed. A feasible estimate of the variance is not provided (need to look into further). The code provided is better than what the literature usually provides, however, it is still hard to follow. It seems that the confidence intervals are constructed by simply using a consistent version (this would make sense).
