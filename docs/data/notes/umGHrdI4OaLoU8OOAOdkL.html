Miscellaneous Ideas

Bonsoo says that the pre-treatment of

Xit=λi′Ft+eitX_{it} = \lambda_i' F_t + e_{it}Xit​=λi′​Ft​+eit​

where FtF_tFt​ itself is allowed to be dynamic. However, the dynamics of FtF_tFt​ do not directly enter the factor structure, and thus this is a called a "static" representation of a dynamic factor model.

Thus, a structural break in XitX_{it}Xit​ may occur in the three broad ways:

  1. A break in λi\lambda_iλi​
  2. A break in FtF_tFt​, which can further be decomposed in
    • A break in the dynamics of FtF_tFt​, such as the change in the coefficients of each lag, or the introduction of more lags
    • A break in the error process of FtF_tFt​
  3. A break in eite_{it}eit​
  4. A break in the intercept term of XitX_{it}Xit​. This is usually omitted from notation, as XitX_{it}Xit​ is assumed to be suitably standardised before analysis

Point 4 has not been focused on too much, but this should be a simple case. If XitX_{it}Xit​ does indeed contain an intercept term, then this is equivalent to a having an additional factors that is constant across time, and the respective loading changing. Therefore, this break is absorbed as a break in the loadings.

Annoyingly, points 1 and 2 are confusing because due to the multiplicative structure of the DFM, breaks in either the loadings or the factors cannot be separately identified.

The entirety of the frequentist literature is focused on breaks in Λ\LambdaΛ, due to the identification issue. This in general is not an issue, because any breaks in the dynamics of FtF_tFt​ can be equivalently represented as a break in the loadings instead.

To see this, we illustrate some examples:

Break in the lag coefficient

Xit=λi′Ft+eit=λi′Ft+eit\begin{aligned} X_{it} &= \lambda_{i}' F_t + e_{it} \\ &= \lambda_{i}' F_t + e_{it} \end{aligned}Xit​​=λi′​Ft​+eit​=λi′​Ft​+eit​​

Emergence (disappearance) of an extra lag

Break in lag coefficient + extra lag

The above three cases should cover all possible break sin the dynamics of FtF_tFt​.

However, there is a gap in what happens when there is a break in the error process of FtF_tFt​.

This point in particular is important, because [@baltagi_identification_2017 (Private)] and [@baltagi_estimating_2021 (Private)] provide the most comprehensive framework in analysing these sort sof structural breaks. Their method is similar to most other methods in the literature thus far, and is essentially analysing the variance process of XitX_{it}Xit​.

Hence, changing our focus from XitX_{it}Xit​ itself to the variance instead, we have.

The variance of XitX_{it}Xit​ breaking could be due to:

  1. Break in the variance covariance of factors
  2. Break in the factor loadings
  3. Break in the variance of the error process

However, their method (and many others) cannot distinguish between a break in the factor loadings and a break in the factor variance due to the identification issue, but can distinguish between this and a break in the error variance.

How it does this is however, is unclear.

New Barigozzi Paper

[@barigozzi_simultaneous_2018 (Private)]'s preprint shows a few exciting results which tie together the EM Kalman Smoother with that of PCA.

Note that the EM estimator is the same as the MLE estimator under homoskedasticity assumptions, and as such, this should be regarded as a QMLE estimator.

In particular, under similar assumption for the PCA estimator, they prove that:

  1. EM has similar convergence rates and consistency as PC
  2. Efficient as PC for loadings
  3. Less efficient than PC for factors

Takeaway: justifies using EM for estimating DFMs, and provides a new, potentially very neat framework for this!

Takeaway:

This is a strong theoretical paper which provides the theoretical justification of the EM algorithm/method of estimating DFMs.

In particular, through the prism of viewing the EM algorithm as a QML estimator, they show that the EM estimator is consistent and surprisingly efficient. Although the assumption is that the errors are homoskedastic, the mis specification only seems to manifest itself in the efficiency of the estimated factors.
