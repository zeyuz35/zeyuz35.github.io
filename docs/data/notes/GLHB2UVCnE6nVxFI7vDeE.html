Empirical Study

Many different empirical studies are possible, given the general nature of the methodology.

For now, focus on the task of inflation forecasting, as this is topical and popular, and there exists a large literature for this. It is also not as controversial to deem forecastable, unlike say, financial data.

There are broadly three macroeconomic indicators that economists tend to care about, and these are all closely related to the functions of a central bank. They are inflation, GDP (economic output) and unemployment ("efficiency" of economic output).

Inflation Forecasting

We consider three different measures of inflation: the personal consumption expenditures (PCE) deflator, the CPI, and core CPI (CPI less food).
