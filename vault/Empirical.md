A collection of so called "stylized facts" in the empirical literature. This is listed in chronological order. 

Note that because these are all stylized facts in economics, these are *all* controversial. Particularly controversial bits are emphasized.

The plus side of this controversy is that if something strange happens in the data, you can come here and pick and choose possible explanations.

Most stylized facts and events are noted here, even if they are a bit minor. The minor events may be useful for counterfactual analysis. 

## Energy Crisis (1973 and 1979)

A supply shock to crude oil, which is an input to almost everything. This resulted in *stagflation*, where these is stagnant growth, but also price inflation. The Keynesian model does not have a good solution to this, and the recommended "solution" is to simply do nothing. 

The do nothing approach was essentially was happened - the world adjusted to higher input costs of oil by innovating and becoming more efficient in petroleum use. In particular, Japanese motors became much more efficient.

## Stock Market Slump (1987)

Japan and West Germany pushed up interest rate, in turn presurring US to push up interest rates, triggering a sell of of shares. 

It is believed that treasurer Keating set out to induce a recession (high unemployment) in order to control inflation. 

In retrospect, the beneficial legacy of this recession was a lasting reduction in inflation.
