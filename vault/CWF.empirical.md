Many different empirical studies are possible, given the general nature of the methodology.

For now, focus on the task of inflation forecasting, as this is topical and popular, and there exists a large literature for this. It is also not as controversial to deem forecastable, unlike say, financial data.

## Inflation Forecasting

We consider three different measures of inflation: the personal consumption expenditures (PCE) deflator, the CPI, and core CPI (CPI less food). Inflation rates are computed via $\pi_t = log(\pi_t - \pi_{t-1})$ (as opposed to the suggested transformation of 2nd log differences in Stock and Watson).
