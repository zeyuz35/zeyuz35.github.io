## Main Quantities

There are generally considered to be 5 quantities of interest (shocks):

1. Unemployment
2. Output (GDP)
3. Hours Works (Productivity)
4. Consumption
5. Investment

How many shocks there are, and whether these shocks are interchangeable, identifiable, etc is a source of great controversy within economics. E.g. ordering of shocks, identification of shocks, etc.

## Frequency Bands

$[1 \pi / 32, 2 \pi / 6]$ is the frequency band typically associated with the business cycle, in terms of quarterly data.

## Stylized Facts

It has been argued that there is little relation between inflation and real economic activity - it is no stronger than the link between asset prices and earnings.

This is strange, given that the New Keynesian model, assuming business cycles are demand drive and the Phillips curve is not exceedingly flat, inflation should be the best predictor of future output gaps.
