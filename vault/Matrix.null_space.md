## Subspaces

A subspace V is defined such that 

- the zero vector is in V
- if u and v are in V< then u + v is also in V
- if v is in V and c is in R, then cv is also in V

## Column Spaces

colspace(A) is the subspace spanned by the columns of A.

If the columns of A are linearly independent, they can form a set of basis vectors.

## Nullspace

nullspace(A) are the set of vectors such that Ax = 0.

nullspace(A) = nullspace(rref(A))

The solution will be in the form of a different number of free variables.

If there are no free variables, then the nullspace of A is just the 0 vector. If this is the case, then the columns of A are linearly independent, by definition.
