Some technical notes for writing.

## Definitions

Proposition is a mathematical statement that is either true or false.

E.g. 3 is greater than 4 is a proposition. 

8 is greater than 7 is a proposition.

Only the first one is true, however. Propositions are also known as conjectures. 

Theorem is a proven proposition. E.g. a^2 + b^2 = c^2 is a proven proposition (Pythgoras theorem), and is therefore a theorem.

Corollary is a result from a theorem that doesn't require too much proof to show. This is more subjective. 

Lemma is a theorem (less than theorem) that needs to be proven, for the purposes of simplifying another proof. This is mostly to keep the actual proofs shorter and easier to follow along.
