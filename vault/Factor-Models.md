# Factor Models

This section gives a broad overview over factor models.

Most of this is more focused on giving a historical overview, and as such as a higher focus on Classical Factor Models. More more relevant and up to date literature, see Static Factor Models (PCA), or Dynamic Factor Models (Dynamic PCA).

## Model Notation

We begin with the simplest factor model representation:

$$
X_{it} = C_{it} + e_{it}
$$

where $X_{it}$ is decomposed into common component $C_{it}$ that is orthogonal to the idiosyncratic error $e_{it}$.

If $e_{it}$ is assumed to strictly independent (mutually orthogonal across all $i, t$), this leads to the so called "exact" or "strong factor model" initially considered by Geweke and Sargent and Sims. Although this, in conjunction with a standard gaussian assumption on the error terms leads to straightfoward estimation via ML/Kalman filter, it is understandably completely unrealistic.
