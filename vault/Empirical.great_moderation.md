Separate section for Great Moderation.

There is no precise date for the Great Moderation, apart from mid 1980s. Wikipedia puts this as 1985 rather arbitrarily.

Some sources put this as 1984. Others provide some evidence for 1982. 

Importantly, the source of a lack of decrease in volatility is mostly observed from only a few key macroeconomic variables. 

Regardless of when exactly this happened, it seems it is quite distinct from the late 1970s. This is more closely associated with the Iranian Revolution which in turn lead to the Second Oil Shock. 

The price of Oil did not return to pre crisis levels until the mid 1980s, again coinciding with Great moderation.

Bernanke's (in)famous speech attributes the Great Moderation to three sources:
  - structural change in the economy
  - improved economic policies
  - good luck

Given that this was given in 2004, comapratively just before the GFC, in retrospect it is considered to be very prematurely self-congratulatory in tone.

Some provide the persepctive that the Great Moderation was simply a tradeoff between risk and stability. That is, the Fed chose to stabilize the economy in the short term in a trade off for potentially larger, long term risk (GFC).
